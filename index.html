Rachel N. Gaines, Ph.D.

Merging electrochemistry, catalysis, and waste valorization for chemical manufacturing

Hi, I'm Rachel, and I electrocute other people's garbage for a living!

More specifically, I am a chemical engineer specializing in complex reaction systems. I use the tools of catalysis, electrochemistry, and engineering to design practial processes for systems that, while critical in industry, are severely understudied at the benchtop scale.

These non-ideal systems provide critical insights at all three scales of chemical process engineering (catalysts, reactions, and reactors) that are cost-effective and scalable to industrial installations. My thesis work has garnered commercial interest from several multinational corporations and led to a successful academic follow-on grant.

I have over a decade of leadership experience from my roles as a research mentor and coach, where I've worked with mentees across wide ranges of age (8-25) and skill. I've served as a project and/or people manager for teams as small as 4 and as large as 31. My mentees have won state and national awards, and secured top Ph.D. program admissions and full-time industry job offers.
